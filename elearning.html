Course Module Redesign

Western Technology Services Project

OVERVIEW

About the Project

Redesigned a Physiotherapy graduate school course to improve design and user engagement.

My Role: Project Lead, UX/UI Design

Tools Used: Articulate Storyline 3, Adobe Illustrator, Jira

Project Context

Our client, a professor from the university's Physiotherapy graduate program approached our department to redesign her course on Articulate Storyline 3. As project lead, I led a team of colleagues and looked through the old modules to research what needed to be improved. From there, we addressed the problems and made a brand guide to keep the design consistent.

Problem

We identified 3 major user experience and design concerns:

    Inconsistent Colours and Theme:
    The colour palette throughout the modules were inconsistent and did not match the official university's colour branding. The layout of each slide varied from one another, therefore we made a template that could be applied across all the slides in the modules.

    Misalignment:
    Graphics, videos, and text were misaligned throughout the slides and looked inconsistent. Having a visually appealing design in eLearning is extremely important for the student's education and how they perceive the material presented.

    Disorganization:
    Slides were poorly organized with no titles or page numbers. When we tested the modules, navigating them was confusing and we were constantly lost. Unorganization in eLearning can negatively impact a student's progress in learning.

CONCLUSION

What I Learned

This project was the first time where I was the project lead. At first, it was challenging to use Jira to organize the project when we all worked different shifts. However, I quickly learned how useful Jira can be for communication. Being a project lead was different from my usual role, I had to learn to delegate tasks and listen to any concerns that my colleagues brought up. I learned that communication with the team was essential in creating a successful product. Overall, being a project lead was scary at first but I quickly learned and felt comfortable in that role.
